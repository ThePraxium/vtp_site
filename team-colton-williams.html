Colton Williams

Executive Director | Founder

Colton is a proud husband, leader, cyber professional, business owner, and founder of The Von Terra Project. Helping others is his life’s work. Over the past 16 years, he has dedicated himself to public service by working to bring stability and understanding to complex, tense, and uncertain environments. His career has spanned national security, leadership, policy making, and cybersecurity, all with a focus on defending critical systems against emerging threats.

Colton’s expertise has taken him across the globe to complex environments, including Guantanamo Bay as an Intelligence NCOIC, Israel as a student of security studies, Kenya as an advisor in developing national defensive cyberspace capabilities, multiple deployments with USCYBERCOM actively countering threats in real-time, and across the country with the National Guard and FEMA partnerships. With a deep passion for security, resilience, and strategic innovation, he continuously seeks to bring together people, policy, and technology.

Education

  • Master of Arts in Security Studies (Concentration in Homeland Defense) — University of Massachusetts, Lowell, MA
  • Bachelor of Arts in Political Science (Minor in Peace and Conflict Studies) — University of Massachusetts, Lowell, MA
  • Certificate – National Security Summit: “Israel’s National Security Challenges in the Changing Middle East” — Israel’s Institute for National Security Studies (INSS), Tel Aviv University, Tel Aviv, Israel
  • Multiple professional certificates from Google, Amazon, Microsoft, PMI, GIAC, CompTIA, EC-Council, FEMA, DCITA, JCITA, UI Path, et al.

Passion & Mission

Colton is driven by the belief that security and stability are the foundations of progress. Whether in cybersecurity, emergency management, or international affairs, his goal is to connect people, policy, and technologies with the knowledge and tools to navigate uncertainty. Through The Von Terra Project, he aims to foster collaboration, education, and actionable solutions to modern security challenges.
